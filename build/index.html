Introduction

Greek mythology consists of stories originally told in ancient Greece. These stories consist of deites, heroes, creatures and the origin of the world itself and have influenced arts and literature of the modern Western society.

Primordial Deites

The gods and goddesses born from the void of Chaos were known as the primordial deites/gods. They gave birth to the some of the gods, the titans and the cyclopses and were eventually overthrown by the gods.

Chaos

Chaos was the the first thing to exist and was known as the abyss/emptiness before the creation of the cosmos. It was thought to be more of a place like Tartarus and some have thought that out from it came the first Titans.

Gaia

Gaia is the personification of the Earth and is as the primal Mother Earth goddess, the ancestral mother of all life. Her son and husband is Uranus and their offspring include the Titans, cyclopses, gigantes and giants.

Uranus

Uranus is the father of the sky and is the son and husband of Gaia. Uranus used to imprison Gaia's youngest children deep within Tartarus until he was deposed of by his son Kronos.

Khronos

Not to be confused with the Titan Kronos, he is known as the father of time, is the consort of Ananke and is depicted as an old, wise man wielding a harvesting scythe.

Ananke

Ananke is the personification of inevitability, compulsion and necessity, she is usually depicted holding a spindle and is considered the most powerful dicatator of fate. Sometimes considered to be the mother of the three Fates (Clotho, Lachesis, Atropos), she is a self-formed being said to have emerged at the dawn of creation in a serpentine form.

Erebus

Erebus is the personification of darkness and is identified as one o the first five beings in existence. He is sometines knwon as a region of the underworld were the dead pass after dying.

Tartarus

In Greek mythology, Tartarus is known as both a deity and a place. It is a deep abyss that is used as a dungeon to torment the wicked and imprison the Titans and with Gaia fathered the serpentine monster Typhon.

Nyx

Commonly depicted as a shadowy figure, Nyx is the personification of the night and was the mother of other personified deites such as Hypnos (sleep) and Thanatos (death) with Erebus. It is said the she holds expectional power which even Zeus himself fears and had protected her child Hypnos from him on multiple occasions.

Titans

The Titans are considered to be the second group of deites after the primordial deites and before the Olympians. The first of the Titans were children of Gaia and Uranus, ruled during the Golden Age and comprised the first pantheon of Greek deites.

Oceanus

The son of Uranus and Gaia, he is considered to be the ocean-stream personified and with his consort Tethys fathered the ocean nymphs (Oceanids) as well as all the rivers, fountains and lakes of the world. He is one of the only titanswho did take the side of the titans (along with Themis and her son Prometheus) in the war Titanomachy and actually withdrew completely from it.

Hyperion

The titan of the heavens, with his sister and wife Theia, he was the father of the titans of the sun, moon and dawn (Helios, Selene and Eos). It is said that he was the first to establish the cycle of days and months, conquered the east corner of Earth and was breaktakingly beautiful. He gave humans the gift of sight.

Theia

Also known as Thea, the eldest daughter of Gaia and Uranus is the titan of light. She was an oracular deity who able to prophesize events and endowed golds, silver and gems with radiance.

Rhea

A member of the first generation of titans, she along with her consort Kronos is the parent of the first of the Olympians and is often referred to as the mother of the gods. While Kronos was swallowing the children she bore, she managed to replace one (Zeus) with a rock to trick him and hid the baby on Mount Ida.

Themis

The titan of divine law and order, she is symbolized with scales which represent balance. She had foresight into the future which enabled her to become one of the Oracles of Delphi and was even addressed as "Lady Themis" by Hera who Themis comforted over her distraught with her husband Zeus.

Mnemosyne

Her name is derived from the same source as the word, mnemonic, she is the titan of memory and rememberance. She mothered the nine muses with her son Zeus after sleeping with him consecutively for the same amount of days and just like her sister titans had oracular abilities.

Kronos

The youngest and ruler of the first of the Titans during the Golden Age, he is usually depicted with a harpe, scythe or sickle. He overthrew his father, the primordial gods Uranus, with the assistance of his mother and was eventually overthrown by his son Zeus, ironically who had the assistance of his wife, Rhea. He is the titan of time and is usually confused with the primordial god of time, Khronos.

Helios

The son of Hyperion and Theia, he is the titan of the sun and and is often confused with Apollo, who is the god of the sun. He is said to ride the sun through the sky encircling the Earth each day using a horse-drawn chariot opposite to his sister Selene.

Selene

The daughter of Hyperion and Theia who was sometimes called Cynthia, she is the titan of the moon and is often confused with Artemis, who is the goddess of the moon. She is said to ride her moon chariot across the heaven and is the only personification of the moon.

Eos

The titan of dawn is said to rise each morning at the edge of Oceanus and is the sister of Helios and Selene. She is married to the titan of the stars Astraeus and rides her chariot across the sky using her horses, Firebright and Daybright, to open the gates of heaven with her rosy fingers to let the sun rise.

Leto

A consort of Zeus and mother of the twins Artemis and Apollo, she is the titan of motherhood and protector of the young. She is said to have a modest and womanly character and along with her twins supported the Trojans during the Trojan war. Whilst pregnant with Zeus' twins she was constantly pursuded by his wife Hera who barred her from many islands and dispatched Ares, the snake dragon Python and others after her. She was only able to give birth to them after her sister, who had previously rejected Zeus, gave her refuge at her island Delos.

Atlas

The titan of endurance, strength and astronomy is the father of the Pleiades, nymphs who were the maiden companions of Artemis. After the Titanomachy, most of the defeated titans were thrown into the pits of Tartarus but Zeus condemned Atlas to stand of the most westerly point on Earth and hold the sky on his shoulders.

Prometheus

His name is may possibily mean forethought, he is the titan who is created with the creation of humans from clay and is considered to a trickster to deites and champion to humankind. It is said that he stole fire from the gods which enabled the progress of human civilization but for that was punished by Zeus to be bound to a rock and eaten by an eagle each day, regenerate overnight and that process was intended to repeat for eternity. He is often linked to the Olypmians Athena and Hephaestus for creativity and technology and was eventually freed from the rock by Heracles.

Metis

Metis meaning wisdom, skill, craft in Greek was the daughter of Oceanus and Tethys and later after marrying Zeus became the goddess of wisdom, prudence and deep thought. It was prophesized that see would give birth to a child so powerful that they would eventually overthrow Zeus and so, he tricked her into turning into a fly and then propmtly swallowed her to prevent that though he was too late, as she was already carrying her first child. Whilst in Zeus' stomach she began creating armour for her child and when she started creating a helmet the hammering got so bad that Zeus asked Hephaestus to split his head open with an axes where her daugther, Athena, came out clad in full armour.

Olympians

The Olympians were a race of deites which consisted of the six of the children of Kronos and Rhea who overthrew the Titans and some of their children, they are the ruled by Zeus, the leader of the second pantheon and resided on Mount Olympus.

Zeus

The king of the gods, ruler of Mount Olympus and god of sky, lightning, thunder, law, order and justice whose main symbol is the thunderbolt he had made by the Cyclopses during Titanomachy. He is very famous for his erotic escapades and as such had divine and heroic offspring including six of the Olympians, also he is known not to fond of humans, frequently attack and hurling lightning bolts at them.

Hera

The goddess of women, marriage, familty and childbirth, she is the sister and wife of Zeus whom was known to cause her much jealously with his many affairs. She is often depicted with cows, lions and peacocks which are animals she considered sacred and riding a chariot drawn by peacocks. It is said that Zeus assigned a nymph named Echo, to distract her with flattery while he went on his escapades and once she found out, cursed Echo be able to repeat the words of others.

Poseidon

The god of the sea, storms, earthquakes and horses, his main symbols is his weapon was a tridents which was crafted by three Cyclopses, as well as various marine creatures. It is said that he created horses out of the foam of the waves and was a caretaker of the prophetic Oracle of Delphi. He resides in a underwater kingdom commonly known as Atlantis.

Demeter

One of the six original gods, she is the goddess of the harvest, plants, agriculture, seasons, fertility and the cycle of life and death. She has many cults worshipping her for bringing plentiful harvests.

Athena

The daughter of Zeus and Metis, born from her father's head in full armour which even causes Helios to stop riding his sun chariot, she is the greek goddess of wisdom, handicraft and war. She is usually depicted with the Owl of Athena (her symbol), the goddess of victory Nike and the sheild Aegis which bears the decapitated head on it.

Apollo

Apollo is the son of Zeus and Leto, god of oracles, music, poetry, dance and archery (which he invented) as well as a patron of Delphi. He is usually depicted with a (golden) bow and (golden) arrows like his sister, is known to offer help warding off evil and protector of the young, concerned with the health and well being of children.

Artemis

The goddess of the hunt, forests, hills, the moon and also archery like her twin brother, she like Athen and Hestia is a maiden goddess and sworn to never marry. Her symbols include a bow and arrow like her brother, as well as a quiver, hunting knives and deers. She is said to be accompanied by an all female troupe of maidens and was worshipped throughout ancient Greece.

Ares

The god of war, he is the son of Zeus and Hera and was known to be very dangerous and destructive. His value as a war god is often placed in doubt as he was on the losing side of the Trojan war while his step sister Athena was on the side of victory and was the lover of the goddess of beauty Aphrodite who was married to Hephaestus. He is often depicted with various weapons, a helmet, chariot and vulture and accompanied by Deimos and Phobos, the gods of terror and fear.

Aphrodite

The Greek goddess of love, beauty, pleasure, passion and sexuality, her symbols include doves, roses, sparrows, swans and myrtles and she is the daugther of Zeus and Dione. She is the unfaithful wife of Hephaestus, is also a warrior goddess and had many cults following her. She is usually accompanied by Eros, as well as the Charites (Graces), Aglaea, Euphrosyne and Thalia, who were also daughters of Zeus.

Hephaestus

The Greek god of blacksmiths, metalwork, carpenters, craftsmen, artisans, sculptors, metallurgy, technology, fire and volcanoes, his symbols include a smith's hammer, anvil and a pair of tongs and is the son of Hera and Zeus. He was cast off Mount Olympus by his mother for his deformity and is said to have created all the weapons of the Olympians with assistance from the Cyclopses that worked at his forges and the automatons he made.

Hermes

The Greek god of messengers, travel, commerce, communication, borders, sports and thieves who is usually depicted with winger sandals, a winget cap and the caduceus which staff with two serpents wrapped around it. He was also called Atlantiades as his mother was the daughter of Atlas and is the possible father of the satyr Pan.

Hestia

The eldest child of Kronos and Rhea and thus the oldest of the Olympians, she is the goddess of the hearth, which is her symbol and the places she resides, as well as home and family. She was sworn to perpetual virginity and such had rejected the likes of Poseidon and Apollo as well as rejecting Aphrodite's values. Among all mortals she was considered the cheif of all the goddess, for every sacrifice to a deity to she received the first offering as well as receiving the first and last libations of wine at feasts. She is known to dislike confrontation and as such eventually gave up her seat as an Olympian to to the more forceful Dionysus as to prevent great conflict between the gods.

Dionysus

The god of wine, grapes, vines, festivity, ecstasy and madness, he is the last and the youngest of the Olympians and the child of Zeus and mortal princess Semele, making him the only Olympian with a mortal mother. It is often disputed how Dionysus became a God since his parentage would make him a demigod and that he some how became a god over time, he was married to the Cretan princess Ariadne.

Other Gods

Other than the twelve Gods who resided on Mount Olypmus, there were numerous other gods that are present through Greek mythology who were children of Primordal deites, Titans and Gods alike.

Circe

Circe is a goddess of magic and the daughter of Hecate and Helios and was well known for her vast knowledge of potions and herbs. She is thought to be a very free spirited deity and would turn those who offended her into animals.

Charon

Working as a ferryman for Hades, he carries the souls of the newly departed across the rivers Styx and Acheron as long as they could pay the fee of one gold coin. He is the son of Erebus and is usually depicted as an unkempt seaman with reddish-brown skin with a pole for his boat.

Eros

Eros is the son of Aphrodite and Ares and along with his siblings the Erotes, was a winged god of love. Like his roman couterpart Cupid, he is depicted as a small flying boy with a bow and arrows as well as a quiver on his back and was regularly thought to be a protector of homosexual love between men.

Hades

The eldest son, fourth child and only child who was swallowed by his father Kronos not considered to be an Olympian, after their victory in the Titanomachy he received rulership of the Underworld. He is often portrayed with Cerberus, the three-headed guard of the Underworld and married his wife throught abduction. Thought to be passive rather than evil, he held the role of maintaining balance above all and with a cold and stern many held all beings equally accountable in accordance to his laws.

Hecate

The goddess og witchcraft, sorcery, crossroads, magic ghosts and necromancy she is known to have a vast knownledge of herbs, poisons and plants to which she also relayed onto her children. Her many cults had thought she had the power to protect cities and homes from the dead and spirits and is usually represented with three appearances, representing full moon, half moon and new moon.

Helios

The son of titans Hyperion and Theia, he, like his sisters, rode a chariot around Earth, his being the sun which was drawn by fiery steads. He is often confused with the sun god Apollo and the daughters of Helios were referred to as the Heliadae.

Hypnos

The origin of the word hypnosis, he is the god of sleep and the son of Nyx and Erebus. He, like his twin Thanatos resided in the Underworld and on various occasions managed to use his powers of trick the king of Gods Zeus through the employment of Hera.

Iris

The daughter of Thaumas and Electra, she is the goddess of rainbows, colours, truths and oaths who played the role of the messengers of the Gods during the Titanomachy. She has golden wings and like Hermes also wields a caduceus and is said to serve nectar to the Gods to drink on Mount Olympus.

Kratos

Kratos is the divine god of strength and like his siblings dwells with Zeus due to his mother having a place in his regime during Titanomachy. He is known to be ruthless and merciless, often mocking other gods for the lack of use of violence as well as supporting Zeus oppressive rule and compelling Hephaestus to chain Prometheus to a rock.

Nike

The sister of Kratos, she is the Greek goddess who personified victory as well as strength and speend, was assigned the role of the divine charioteer during the Titanomachy. She is said to speed across battlefields rewarding victors with glory and fame and was depicted as a close acquaintance of Athena, having a fairy like appearance with two wings on her back.

Pan

The greek god of the wild, shepherds, flocks and impromptus, his has the hindquarters, legs and horns of a goat, giving him the appearance of a satyr. His name with the deriviation of the word panic and his places of worship were usually in caves and grottoes rather than temples, is the son of Hermes and has a horrible screech said to be able to halt any battle.

Persephone

Also known as Kore, she is the daughter of Zeus and Demeter and the goddess of springtime, flowers, vegetation and after her abduction and marriage by Hades, the underworld. Her primary symbol is the pomegranate which Hades tricked her into eating which left her unable to leave the Underworld for a third of the year, which is how the winter months to occur.

Triton

The son of Poseidon and Amphitrite, he was the ruler of the depths of the sea and represented as a merman with the upper body of a human and lower body of a fish. He lived with his parents at their golden palace at the bottom of the sea, is portrayed as a messenger for Poseidon and like his father also wields a trident. Deeply associated with conch shells, it is said he blew them like trumpets or horns to calm the seas and was the foster parent of Athena whom he raised alongside his daughter Pallas.

Demigods

Demigods were the children of gods and mortals, who obtained various powers from their immortal parents but unlike them, were not immortal and were able to eventually die, through injuries, diseases, ageing and other causes.

Achilles

A hero who participated in the Trojan war, he is the son of Thetis and Peleus and is depicted wielding a spear and the Sheild of Achilles, which was made by Hephaestus through the persuasion of his mother. He body was completely invulnerable apart from his heel as his mother dipped him in the river Styx as an infant and only his left heel was not submerged.

Helen

Known s Helen of Sparta or Helen of Troy, she is said to be the most beautiful women in the hold and is the daughter of Zeus and Leda. After being promised to by Aphrodite, she was abducted by Paris of Troy whilst she was married to King Menelaus of Sparta which resulted in the Trojan war.

Heracles

Thought to be one of the greatest hero in Greek Mythology, he is the son of Zeus and Alcmene and half-brother and great-grandson of Perseus. He had magnificent strength, courage as well as wit when he was in battle and is usually depicted with a club and the skin from the Nemean lion. Even at only eight months old, he managed to strangle two giant snakes to death with each hand and is famous for the ten labours he completed, which thereafter made him a god and granted him immortality.

Hippolyta

The queen of the brutal female warriors, the Amazons, who resided in Asia Minor, she was the daughter of Harmonia and Area, the latter of which granted her with a magical girdle. She is said to be so impressed with Heracles that she willingly gave her girdle to help to assist with one of his labours without argument.

Minos

The first king of Crete and son of Zeus and Eupora, he is said to send seven boys and seven girls the Labyrinth, to be eaten by the Minotaur. He was eventually killed when he demanded Daedalus to be handed over by King Cocalus and the king conviced them to take a bath first which then Daedalus and the Cocalus' daughters drowned him. After his death he became a judge of a death in Hades' court.

Orpheus

Orpheus was a great poet, prophet and most notable of all musician and is said to be all them charm all living things as well as stones with his music. After the death of his wife Eurydice, he began to such sad songs which made all listeners weep and so on their advice, he travelled to the Underworld to bring her back. He managed to softened the hearts of Hades and Persephone who allowed them to return to the surface as long as he walked in front of her and didn't turn back until they were outside. He followed they're instructions perfectly until he got outside, where in his haste, turned around while she was steps away from the upper world, losing her forever.

Perseus

The legendary founder of Mycenae, beheader of the Gorgon Medusa and rescuer of Andromeda from the sea monster Cetus, he was the son of Zeus and Danaë. His name comes from the Greek verbs, to ravage and was said to have slain many great enemies and used the Gorgon's head he possessed to turn them to stone.

Monsters

In Greek mythology there are many monsters created through various circumstances, that had were not considered human though some had human-like features and many had terrifying appearances.

Arachne

She was an extremely talented weaver who was a mortal and started her talent at a very early age as the daughter of a shepherd. As she grew old she got quite boastful even insinuating that her skills her even greater than Athena's who then challenged her to a contest. Once finished Athena's piece showed contests between mortals and Gods and the former being punished for daring to see them as equals, Arachne's piece on the other hand showed the ways Gods had wronged and abused humans. Seeing that her opponents work was not only greater than her's but also insulted the Gods, Athena became enraged, ripping Arachne's work and hitting her on the head three times, making Arachne so terrified that she hanged herself. Before she died Athena said "Live on then, yet hang, condemned own" and after sprinkling her with juice from Hecate's herb, Arachne immediately lost all her hair along with her nose and ears and her head, body and limbs shrank significantly, making her a spider and forced to weaved her web.

Centaur

Sometimes known as Hippocentaur, they were mythological creatures with the upper body of humans and lower body of horses who were said to inhabite regions in Thessaly, Elis and Laconia. Centaurs are said to be greatly skilled with bows and arrows as symbol of the star sign Sagittarius is a half-man, half-horse wield those. Notable centaurs include:

Centaurus
The father of all centaurs and child of Ixion and Nephele who was said to guide the Argonauts as a constellation.
Chiron
The king of all centaurs, he was known as the wisest and justest of his species, who also tutored the likes of Heracles. He was the son of Kronos and Philyra and was taught by Apollo.

Cerberus

The three-headed guardian of the gates of the Underworld, it was also known as the hound of Hades and had the tail of a serpent and multiple snakes coming from its body. It is the child of Echidna and Typhon.

Chimera

The Chimera also knownas Chimaera, were monstrous fire breathing creatures usually depicted as a lion with a snake as a tail and a goat's head protuding from its back though it an usually mean any mythical or fictional animal made from many other animals.

Cyclops

The Cyclopes were single-eyed giant creatures, were master craftmens forging many great weapons and many were the assistants Hephaestus in his forges. They were also the brothers of the Hekatoncheires and the Titans and were known to have created some of the most powerful weapons. Notable Cyclopes include:

Arges
He along with his two brothers, Brontes and Steropes created Zeus' iconic thunderbolt
Polyphemus
A savage man-eating being who was tricked by Odysseus who fleed after blinding him and convincing Polyphemus his name was Nobody.

Drakon

The deriviation of the mythical beasts dragons, these were serpent-like creatures who usually had the ability to produce harmful substances. Notable drakon's include:

Ladon
A wingless dragon which was twisted around a tree to protect golden apples for the Hesperiades which was overcome by Heracles.
Lernaean Hydra
A serpent-like water-dwelling monster which was located at Lerna, one of the entrances to the Underworld, who grew two heads each times another was cut.
Python
The giant serpent which lived at the center of the Earth, thought to have the appearance of modern dragons which eventually slain by Apollo.
Typhon
The serpentine offspring of Gaia and Tartarusn it is known as own of the most deadly creatures in Greek mythology and the father of all other drakons as well as many other monster with the mother of monsters Echidna.

Echidna

Echidna was a half-human half-snake monster who was the consort of Typhon and gave birth to many monsters in Greek mythology such as Cerberus, the Spinx and the some of the Drakons. She dwelled in a deep cave beneath the secret parts of Earth away from mortals and gods and killed any being which dared pass by.

Empusa

The Empousai were female shape-shifting monsters who each possessed a single leg made out of copper and were commanded by the Goddess of witchcraft Hecate. It Late Antiquity, they were thought to be similar to phantoms and spectres and to seduce and feed on young men.

The Fates

The Fates, were three white-robed females who were the incarnations of destiny and were said to appear within three days of a babies' birth to decide its fate. They are said to be the commanded by Zeus and conrol the thread of life, were credited with the creation of the alphabet alongside Hermes and are as known as the Moirai. The names of the Fates were:

Atropos
The oldest of the three Fates, she is also the Goddess of fate and destiny and was the one who cut the thread, giving her the task of ending the life of all mortals.
Clotho
The youngest of the three Fates, she is responsible for the thread of life and spins it out and also has the ability to create life for all mortals.
Lachesis
The second of the three Fates, she is normally depicted in all white clothing and measured the thread, giving her the ability to determine the length of life for all mortals.

The Gorgons

The Gorgons were a group of three sister who were daughters of Phorcys and Ceto, two of which were immortal and all had hair which was made out of venomous poison and direct gaze could turn those would look into it to stone. Their names were Stheno, Euryale and Medusa and were said to have wings, claws, boar tusks and scaly skin.

Harpy

Harpies were thought to be female creatures with the top half of a human and the bottom half of a bird. They were said to agents of punishment who viciously snatch evil-doers from the ground and torture them as the carried them to Tartarus with the sharp claws.

Hekatoncheires

The Hekatoncheires (Hundered-Handed Ones), also known as Centimanes, were the three monstrous giant offspring of Gaia and Uranus who each had fifty heads and one hundred arms and helped the Olympians overthrow the Titans. Their names were Briareus, Cottus and Gyges and they had incredible strength.

Minotaur

The Minotaur is a legendary beast born from, with the head and tails of a bull and body of human which dwelled at the center of the Labyrinth and was born from Pasiphaë, the wife of Minos and a bull. It is said to wield a giant one-handed, double bit axe called a Labrys, slay all mortals who entered the Labyrinth and was eventually slain by the hero Theseus.

Nemean Lion

One of the offspring of Echidna and Typhon, it was said to not be killable with mortal weapons as its golden fur made it impervious to attacks from the and it had claws that could cut through any armour. It was given its name as it was sent to Nemea to terrorize the city and was killed by Heracles when he strangled it with his bare hands.

Nymph

Nymphs were divine female spirits who maintained the natural environments they lived in, such as the air, sea, woods, waters and sands and are depicted as beautiful, graceful women. They were not immortal but were said to live for many years and were generally thought to be part of the retinue of Artemis. Types of nymphs include:

Alseid
Nymphs which resided in glens and grooves.
Auloniad
Nymphs which resided in mountains pastures and vales, often the companions of the god of nature, Pan.
Crinaeae
Nymphs which resided in fountains, wells and other bodies of water.
Dryads
Nymphs who were tree spirits and resided within them or be hybrids of them, they were known to be very shy except around the goddess of Artemis.
Eleionomae
Nymphs which resided in marshes and wetlands.
Hamadryads
Nymphs which were born bonded to a certain tree and had their life linked to it, for this reason they are said to punish any mortal who harmed trees.
Hesperides
Nymphs of the evening and sunset, also known as Atlantides as they were born from the Titan Atlas.
Hyades
A sisterhood of nymphs which brought rain, they were also daughters of Atlas and dwelled near bodies of water.
Lampads
Nymphs which resided in the Underworld, they were a gift from Zeus to, whom they were the companions of, Hecate and bear torches which could drive beings to madness for her as she travelled throught the night.
Limnades
Nymphs which resided in freshwater lakes and were the children of river or lake gods.
Meliae
Nymphs of the ash tree from which they share their name, they were born from the drops of blood which fell on Gaia when Kronus castrated Uranus and were the nurses of Zeus when he was an infant.
Napaeae
Nymphs which resided in wooden environments such as valleys, glens and grottoes.
Nereids
Nymphs which resided in the sea, they were the daughters of Nereus and Doris and the companions of Poseidon and were friendly and helpful to those lost at sea.
Oceanids
Nymphs which resided in the ocean, they were the three thousan daugthers of the Titans Oceanus and Tethys.
Oreads
Nymphs which resided in the mountains who were very aggressive and associated which the Goddess of the Hunt, Artemis, whom they help while she was out in the mountains, her preferred hunting place.
Pegaeae
Nymphs which resided in springs, the were the great aunts of the river gods and established the relationship between springs and rivers.
Pegasides
Nymphs which resided in wells and springs, they were given that name as they were created when the mythical horse Pegasus struck its hooves on the ground
Pleiades
Nymphs which resided in the sea, they were the daughters of Atlas and the another sea nymph Pleione, they were trained by Artemis, the maids, nurses and teachers of Dionysus and were thought to be place upon the night sky as clusters of stars after death.
Potamides
Nymphs which resided in bodies of freshwater, which were favourable to young women and aggressive to men, dragging their bodies under their abodes.
Thriae
The three sisters Melaina, Kleodora and Daphnis, they were the nymphs of the sacred springs of Corycian cave and patrons of bees.

Phoenix

Heavily associated with the Sun, the phoenix was a large bird made from fire. Said to be immortal, once it died it obtains new life from rises out of the ashes of its previous form which combusted on death. It is used to symbolize renewal, the sun, time, an empire, paradise, virginity, metempsychosis, consecration and resurrection.

Pegasus

The Pegasus is a mythical winged horse, the offspring of Poseidon and was used to symbolize wisdom and fame. Everytime it strikes its hooves against the ground a spring is created, it carries Zeus' thunderbolts for him when he goes into battle and was later transformed by Zeus into a constellation across the skies.

Satyr

A Satyr is a male nature spirit with the top half of a human and bottom half of a goat, as well as having horse ears and a tail. The companions of the Olympian Dionysus and Pan were known for their love of wine, music, dancing and women and dwelled in remote locations such as woodlands, mountains and pastures. They were said to be skilled at playing the woodwind instruments such as pan flutes and were widely known as mischievous creatures often playing pranks on mortals, monsters and Gods alike. Notable satyrs include:

Marsyas
An expert aulos player, he once challenged Apollo to a music contest in which the winner could treat the loser however they wanted. Marsyas' music produced by his flute caused everyone there to go into a frenzy and dance wildly, Apollo's music caused all that heard the lyre halt to a standstill and gush tears. Marsyas defeated once Apollo added his voice to his song and was declared the winner by the Muses who were judging the contest and Apollo choose to havo him flayed alive and nail his skin to a pine tree which he later repented on the excessiveness of his actions.
Silenus
He was the tutor and companion of the God of wine Dionysus, considered to be one of the oldest of the Satyrs. When drunk he is said to possess extraordinary wisdom and prophetical powers and as such a was cursed to be eternally drunk.

Siren

Siren were female creatures with the scaly feet, wings and feathers who had beautiful appearances and voices most of which were the daughters of the river God Achelous. They were said to lurk in the sea, where the lured sailors which their enchanting voices causing them to shipwreck and the subsequently ate them, fated to die if a mortal escaped from them and the previous companions of the Goddess Persephone.

Sphinx

A female merciless beast with the head of a human, wings of an eagle, serpent tail and remaining body of a lion it is said to ask however crossed its path a riddle and if it was not solved they would be killed and eaten. It is notoriously linked with ancient Egypt were many structures were based on its appearance.
