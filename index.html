Introduction

Greek mythology consists of stories originally told in ancient Greece. These stories consist of deites, heroes, creatures and the origin of the world itself and have influenced arts and literature of the modern Western society.

Deites

In Greek mythology beings that could be harmed but would regenerate and be unabled to die were known as deites (immortals, divine figures). Deites are usually split into fives categories: Primordial deties, Titans, Olympians, minor gods and Gigantes/Giants.

Primordial Deites

The gods and goddesses born from the void of Chaos were known as the primordial deites/gods. They gave birth to the some of the gods, the titans and the cyclopses and were eventually overthrown by the gods.

Chaos

Chaos was the the first thing to exist and was known as the abyss/emptiness before the creation of the cosmos. It was thought to be more of a place like Tartarus and some have thought that out from it came the first Titans.

Gaia

Gaia is the personification of the Earth and is as the primal Mother Earth goddess, the ancestral mother of all life. Her son and husband is Uranus and their offspring include the Titans, cyclopses, gigantes and giants.

Uranus

Uranus is the father of the sky and is the son and husband of Gaia. Uranus used to imprison Gaia's youngest children deep within Tartarus until he was deposed of by his son Kronos.

Khronos

Not to be confused with the Titan Kronos, he is known as the father of time, is the consort of Ananke and is depicted as an old, wise man wielding a harvesting scythe.

Ananke

Ananke is the personification of inevitability, compulsion and necessity, she is usually depicted holding a spindle and is considered the most powerful dicatator of fate. Sometimes considered to be the mother of the three Fates (Clotho, Lachesis, Atropos), she is a self-formed being said to have emerged at the dawn of creation in a serpentine form.

Erebus

Erebus is the personification of darkness and is identified as one o the first five beings in existence. He is sometines knwon as a region of the underworld were the dead pass after dying.

Tartarus

In Greek mythology, Tartarus is known as both a deity and a place. It is a deep abyss that is used as a dungeon to torment the wicked and imprison the Titans and with Gaia fathered the serpentine monster Typhon.

Nyx

Commonly depicted as a shadowy figure, Nyx is the personification of the night and was the mother of other personified deites such as Hypnos (sleep) and Thanatos (death) with Erebus. It is said the she holds expectional power which even Zeus himself fears and had protected her child Hypnos from him on multiple occasions.

Titans

The Titans are considered to be the second group of deites after the primordial deites and before the Olympians. The first of the Titans were children of Gaia and Uranus, ruled during the Golden Age and comprised the first pantheon of Greek deites.

Oceanus

The son of Uranus and Gaia, he is considered to be the ocean-stream personified and with his consort Tethys fathered the ocean nymphs (Oceanids) as well as all the rivers, fountains and lakes of the world. He is one of the only titanswho did take the side of the titans (along with Themis and her son Prometheus) in the war Titanomachy and actually withdrew completely from it.

Hyperion

The titan of the heavens, with his sister and wife Theia, he was the father of the titans of the sun, moon and dawn (Helios, Selene and Eos). It is said that he was the first to establish the cycle of days and months, conquered the east corner of Earth and was breaktakingly beautiful. He gave humans the gift of sight.

Theia

Also known as Thea, the eldest daughter of Gaia and Uranus is the titan of light. She was an oracular deity who able to prophesize events and endowed golds, silver and gems with radiance.

Rhea

A member of the first generation of titans, she along with her consort Kronos is the parent of the first of the Olympians and is often referred to as the mother of the gods. While Kronos was swallowing the children she bore, she managed to replace one (Zeus) with a rock to trick him and hid the baby on Mount Ida.

Themis

The titan of divine law and order, she is symbolized with scales which represent balance. She had foresight into the future which enabled her to become one of the Oracles of Delphi and was even addressed as "Lady Themis" by Hera who Themis comforted over her distraught with her husband Zeus.

Mnemosyne

Her name is derived from the same source as the word, mnemonic, she is the titan of memory and rememberance. She mothered the nine muses with her son Zeus after sleeping with him consecutively for the same amount of days and just like her sister titans had oracular abilities.

Kronos

The youngest and ruler of the first of the Titans during the Golden Age, he is usually depicted with a harpe, scythe or sickle. He overthrew his father, the primordial gods Uranus, with the assistance of his mother and was eventually overthrown by his son Zeus, ironically who had the assistance of his wife, Rhea. He is the titan of time and is usually confused with the primordial god of time, Khronos.

Helios

The son of Hyperion and Theia, he is the titan of the sun and and is often confused with Apollo, who is the god of the sun. He is said to ride the sun through the sky encircling the Earth each day using a horse-drawn chariot opposite to his sister Selene.

Selene

The daughter of Hyperion and Theia who was sometimes called Cynthia, she is the titan of the moon and is often confused with Artemis, who is the goddess of the moon. She is said to ride her moon chariot across the heaven and is the only personification of the moon.

Eos

The titan of dawn is said to rise each morning at the edge of Oceanus and is the sister of Helios and Selene. She is married to the titan of the stars Astraeus and rides her chariot across the sky using her horses, Firebright and Daybright, to open the gates of heaven with her rosy fingers to let the sun rise.

Leto

A consort of Zeus and mother of the twins Artemis and Apollo, she is the titan of motherhood and protector of the young. She is said to have a modest and womanly character and along with her twins supported the Trojans during the Trojan war. Whilst pregnant with Zeus' twins she was constantly pursuded by his wife Hera who barred her from many islands and dispatched Ares, the snake dragon Python and others after her. She was only able to give birth to them after her sister, who had previously rejected Zeus, gave her refuge at her island Delos.

Atlas

The titan of endurance, strength and astronomy is the father of the Pleiades, nymphs who were the maiden companions of Artemis. After the Titanomachy, most of the defeated titans were thrown into the pits of Tartarus but Zeus condemned Atlas to stand of the most westerly point on Earth and hold the sky on his shoulders.

Prometheus

His name is may possibily mean forethought, he is the titan who is created with the creation of humans from clay and is considered to a trickster to deites and champion to humankind. It is said that he stole fire from the gods which enabled the progress of human civilization but for that was punished by Zeus to be bound to a rock and eaten by an eagle each day, regenerate overnight and that process was intended to repeat for eternity. He is often linked to the Olypmians Athena and Hephaestus for creativity and technology and was eventually freed from the rock by Heracles.

Metis

Metis meaning wisdom, skill, craft in Greek was the daughter of Oceanus and Tethys and later after marrying Zeus became the goddess of wisdom, prudence and deep thought. It was prophesized that see would give birth to a child so powerful that they would eventually overthrow Zeus and so, he tricked her into turning into a fly and then propmtly swallowed her to prevent that though he was too late, as she was already carrying her first child. Whilst in Zeus' stomach she began creating armour for her child and when she started creating a helmet the hammering got so bad that Zeus asked Hephaestus to split his head open with an axes where her daugther, Athena, came out clad in full armour.
